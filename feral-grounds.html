The Feral Grounds

A future community project for Miyawaki-inspired native micro-forests, small habitat corridors, and rehabilitating overlooked urban spaces into living ground again.

Image placeholder

The Idea

Community care for city soil.

The Feral Grounds will be heavily influenced by the Miyawaki method: dense, layered native plant communities, improved soil, and small urban sites planted with the intention of becoming self-supporting habitat over time.

Early Focus

Small sites, dense native systems.

01

Miyawaki-Inspired Patches

Small, dense native plantings designed with canopy layers, understory texture, and long-term habitat value.

02

Soil & Succession

Preparing compacted or tired urban ground so native plant communities can establish, compete, and mature.

03

Community Stewardship

Volunteer-ready planting days, simple care plans, and visible progress neighbors can participate in.
